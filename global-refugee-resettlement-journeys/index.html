Refugee resettlement is a process in which refugees who have fled conflict, persecution, or violence are relocated from the country where they initially sought asylum to a third country that has agreed to provide them with legal protection and a permanent home. Resettlement typically occurs when refugees are unable to find safety, have specific needs that cannot be met in the country where they first sought refuge, or are unable to return to their country of origin due to ongoing conflict, persecution, or violence. In these cases, UNHCR assists them in relocating to a third country.

In 2023, the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) assisted in resettling 96,311 refugees across various nations. However, the resettlement process remains slow-moving and limited, with less than 1% of refugees resettled each year. This analysis focuses on the pathways followed by refugees, including their country of origin, the country of asylum where they initially sought refuge, and the country where they are ultimately resettled.

The Intricate Web of Refugee Resettlement

This network diagram visualizes the global movement of refugees in 2023, with each circle representing a country involved in the resettlement process. The green lines show the journey from the country of origin to a country of asylum, while the purple lines depict the movement from country of asylum to country of resettlement.

Each line represents the journey of anywhere from 1 to approximately 9,500 refugees, with thicker lines representing routes with larger numbers of refugees. This network highlights the complex and connected paths of refugee journeys around the world.

 

Individual Journeys from a Common Country of Origin

Each resettlement journey is unique, shaped by a variety of factors like personal circumstances, geopolitical events, and available pathways. These journeys typically start with a search for safety, followed by a complex asylum process, and then a second complex resettlement process. Below is a visual representation of data showing how refugees moved from their countries of origin, through countries of asylum, and to their resettlement destinations in 2023. As refugees move through the resettlement process, their new location becomes increasingly permanent, and the distance from their country of origin continues to grow.
