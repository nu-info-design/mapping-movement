Disaster-Induced Internal Displacements in 2023: How Many People Were Forced to Move in 2023 and Why?

Chaitanya Sai Botta

This site lets you explore the global impact of disasters like floods, storms, earthquakes, and wildfires. Through interactive maps and charts, you’ll see where people were displaced and what caused it. The data is real, the visuals are clear, and there’s no clutter—just a powerful way to understand how natural events are changing lives around the world.

Global Refugee Resettlement Journeys: 2023

Jordan Gilardi

In 2023, the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) assisted in resettling 96,311 refugees. This visualization experience begins with a global network diagram that highlights the complex, interconnected pathways of refugee resettlement. The second part features custom interactive visualizations that trace the unique movement of refugees from their countries of origin, through countries of asylum, to their final resettlement destinations. The aim of this project is to provide insights into refugee resettlement and the ongoing search for safety on both a global and country scale.

Global Scholar Relocations

Haolan Z

Academic communication is largely made possible by the mobility of scholars around the world. Over the last century, more than 10,000,000 scholars have moved from place to place for their own reasons, emerging countless patterns and phenomena of different dimensions. Inspired by MPIDR's Scholarly Migration Database and the dataset it collected in 2023, this project presents an interactive visualization focused on the relocation flows of scholars worldwide, offering you the opportunity to explore this sophisticated database in a much more tangible way.

Internal Displacement During Wartime in Ukraine

Jane S

Explore the evolving landscape of internal displacement in Ukraine over the past three years of war—and discover how the very methods for measuring it have advanced during that time.

Ripples Into Silence: Lives That Vanished Within Sight of Lampedusa

Zhuoqi Liu

Ripples into Silence visualizes the lives lost near the shores of Lampedusa. Each animation uses real data to trace the final journey of a migrant who never reached safety. As more paths appear and fade, a quiet sense of loss builds, reminding us that each number represents a life once filled with hopes, dreams, and a story. The project invites viewers to reflect on the ongoing crisis at Europe’s border and to care more deeply about the people whose stories remain unheard.

The H-1B Game

Gab K De Jesus

In FY 2024, there were over 780,000 H-1B Visa applications. While most students are aware of the process, H-1B results aren’t usually synthesized to inform applicants—leaving them in the dark about what the timeline and chances look like.

Vanishing Journeys: Tragedy and Survival on the Path to the U.S.

Joy Zhang

Vanishing Journeys is a data-driven narrative that brings visibility to the unseen human stories of migration toward the United States. Through a rotating visual field of incidents, the project sheds light on where migrants come from, what risks they face, and who survives—or doesn’t. With interactive elements reflecting survivor rates, gender imbalance, and causes of death, it transforms cold data into a compassionate exploration of displacement. This project is both a tribute and a reckoning—a reminder that behind every data point is a journey interrupted, a family left waiting, and a voice that deserves to be heard.

Where are Tuvaluans Going?

Carmen Quintos

This project highlights Tuvalu, a Pacific island nation often overlooked in Western media, yet pioneering a transition to becoming the world’s first digital nation. It provides essential context about Tuvalu’s geography, population, and vulnerability to climate change. Through visualizations, this exploration traces the impacts of rising sea levels on both the land and its people, featuring observed internal displacement and documented international migration trends. By combining data with speculative futures, it illustrates how environmental events are shaping the Tuvaluan experience. This work aims to elevate awareness and understanding of Tuvalu’s unique situation in the broader global climate and migration discourse.

Produced by students in the Northeastern University Information Design and Data Visualization programs in 2025.
